About CS370

370 is a course for aspiring teachers. It provides guidance to Lab Assistants, introducing them to the Big Ideas of Learning and Teaching, and how to put them into practice. The course is roughly divided into two halves. The first half covers how to interact with students one-on-one. This is directly applicable to lab assistants who typically operate in these settings. The second half covers how to interact with a group of students (typically what a TA is responsible for). Class is discussion based, and covers both practical and pedagogical aspects to teaching Computer Science. The main value of the course lies in the weekly tutoring sessions (detailed below).

Prerequisites

Have taken any of the following (and are interested in teaching it): CS{10, 61{A/AS/B/C}, 70}. We expect each student to be lab assisting for the class they're interested in helping concurrently with 370. If you aren't planning on lab assisting, please email Alfonso about enrollment. All current TAs and readers are welcome.

Time Commitment

The key weekly assignment in this class is the tutoring session. For Summer 2015, you will expect to do this for 5 weeks total (8 weeks minus first two weeks and last week).

Each student taking 370 will be expected to hold individual on predetermined topics with students from the class they are lab assisting/reading for. 370 Students are expected to do at least three hours a week. The students being tutored will give specific, structured feedback on how the lab assistant is doing. This includes:

This includes:

  • Demeanor / supportiveness
  • Knowledge of the course material
  • Communicating effectively
  • Providing the right amount of input or guidance

The hope is that constant, weekly feedback will allow students to improve their skills meaningfully in teaching and guiding students.

There are other readings or writing assignments each week. These will take no more than an 30 minutes a week to complete.

The total time commitment per week is 7 hours:

  • 2 hours sections
  • 2 hour homework
  • 3 hours tutoring
  • 1 hour prep for tutoring
